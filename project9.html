Unannounced Project

NDA Protected Team Development & Modular Prototyping

Project Introduction

This is a professional/academic team project currently under a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). While specific narrative details and IP elements cannot be shared, my role centered on establishing the modular level design pipeline, rapid whiteboxing, and coordinating with the engineering team to ensure our spatial designs met the strict technical constraints of the engine.

My Contributions

  • Modular Blockout Toolkit: Developed a standardized grid-snapping library of whitebox assets to speed up iteration times across the entire level design team.
  • Technical Constraints Management: Monitored draw calls and polycounts during the greybox phase, implementing occlusion culling strategies early in development.
  • Systems Integration: Placed and tested early gameplay system hooks (e.g., enemy spawn volumes, quest triggers) before final art was locked.
  • Documentation: Wrote comprehensive Level Design Documents (LDDs) outlining intended player flow, encounter setups, and specific asset requests for the environment art team.

Design Analysis

Working under strict creative and technical constraints required a highly organized approach to level structure. The core design philosophy I championed was "Form Follows Function." Before any aesthetic decisions were made, every metric—from the height of cover objects to the width of main thoroughfares—was mathematically verified to support the core combat loop.

Because the project details are confidential, I emphasize the *process*. The ability to rapidly tear down and rebuild a section of the map based on weekly milestone feedback without breaking the underlying technical logic was the key achievement in this endeavor.
