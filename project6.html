Beast Motos

Tabletop Board Game & System Design

Project Introduction

Beast Motos is an original tabletop board game that focuses on resource management, strategic positioning, and player-driven market dynamics. Creating a physical game requires a deep understanding of core systems without relying on digital processing power, making it a pure test of game logic and numerical balance.

My Contributions

  • Core Loop Design: Established the foundational gameplay loops—gathering resources, upgrading vehicles (motos), and competing for spatial dominance on the board.
  • Paper Prototyping: Iterated through multiple physical prototypes using index cards and tokens to test the fun-factor of mechanics before finalizing rules.
  • Rulebook Writing: Authored clear, edge-case-proof rulebooks to ensure players could onboard themselves without external guidance.
  • Numerical Balancing: Utilized spreadsheets to calculate probability distributions for dice rolls and card draws, ensuring no dominant "meta" strategy broke the game.

Design Analysis

The biggest challenge in tabletop design is "Mental Math Reduction"—ensuring that the complex interactions between players don't result in tedious calculations. In Beast Motos, I solved this by using a physical "Market Tracker" board that visually represents supply and demand, offloading the cognitive load from the players to the game pieces.

Additionally, the game employs a "Catch-up Mechanic." Through iterative playtesting, I noticed early leaders tended to snowball. I introduced dynamic event cards that slightly penalize hoarders while rewarding aggressive risk-takers, keeping the tension high until the final round.
