GMTK 2024: Build to Scale

Puzzle Platforming & Dynamic Environment Sizing

Project Introduction

Participating in the highly competitive GMTK Game Jam 2024 under the theme "Built to Scale." We created a puzzle platformer where the player can dynamically alter the scale of environmental objects to create bridges, block hazards, or reach new heights. This required extreme precision in level metrics and puzzle conceptualization.

My Contributions

  • Puzzle Design: Blocked out 5 distinct puzzle rooms, each introducing a new application of the scaling mechanic (e.g., scaling a block to fit a gap vs. scaling a block to gain mass/weight).
  • Boundary Control: Implemented strict bounding boxes to prevent players from scaling objects infinitely, which would break the physics engine and the game's progression.
  • Visual Affordance: Collaborated with artists to ensure scalable objects were instantly recognizable against the static background elements.

Design Analysis

Designing levels where the player can change the geometry of the level is inherently risky, as it invites sequence breaking. My solution was a "Lock and Key Topology." The physical space of the room itself acts as the lock, while the scalable block is the key.

For example, a puzzle might require the player to squeeze a block through a tiny crevice (min scale), and then expand it on the other side to act as a staircase (max scale). By tightly controlling the dimensions of the crevices and the platforms, I ensured the "Eureka" moment of the puzzle was preserved despite the free-form nature of the mechanic.
