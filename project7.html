Petalnaut

Collaborative Level Design & Environmental Storytelling

Project Introduction

Petalnaut is a team-based development project where I served as the primary Level Designer. The game features a unique aesthetic blending botanical themes with exploration mechanics. My primary goal was to construct environments that naturally communicated the lore of the world while seamlessly accommodating the player's traversal abilities.

My Contributions

  • Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration: Worked closely with Environment Artists to transition functional whiteboxes into fully realized, aesthetically cohesive levels.
  • Whiteboxing & Metrics: Established core metrics for jump distances, character bounds, and camera constraints to ensure smooth traversal.
  • Asset Implementation: Managed the placement of set-dressing assets and collision volumes to optimize performance and prevent sequence breaking.
  • Version Control (Perforce/Git): Maintained a clean repository structure, avoiding map conflicts during concurrent team development.

Design Analysis

The design philosophy for Petalnaut relied heavily on "Landmark Navigation." In a visually rich and colorful world, it's easy for players to suffer from sensory overload. I designed the macro-layout around massive, distinct botanical structures that served as clear points of reference, acting as a compass for the player.

During the iteration phase, playtesting revealed that players often got lost in denser sections. To solve this, I collaborated with the lighting artists to implement a "light-guiding" system—using subtle atmospheric lighting and saturated flora to draw the player's eye toward critical path exits naturally.
