Escape from Power Plant

Half-Life 2 Custom Mod & Linear FPS Level Design

Project Introduction

Escape from Power Plant is a classic, narrative-driven linear FPS level built using the Source Engine's Hammer Editor. The goal of this project was to master the fundamentals of traditional level design pacing—specifically, the ebb and flow of tension, enemy encounters, and environmental puzzle-solving inherent to the Half-Life 2 formula.

My Contributions

  • Hammer Editor Greyboxing: Blocked out the entire power plant facility, ensuring player paths were intuitive yet challenging.
  • Visual Scripting & Triggers: Set up complex I/O entities (Input/Output) to trigger explosive barrels, collapsing structures, and synchronized enemy ambushes.
  • Combat Encounter Pacing: Placed Combine soldiers and zombies in strategic locations to create distinct arenas with varying tactical requirements.
  • Lighting for Guidance: Utilized the Source Engine's lighting system to subtly guide the player's eye toward critical paths and health pickups.

Design Analysis

The layout follows a strict pacing curve: Build-up, Climax, and Resolution. I focused heavily on "Sightline Control"—ensuring that the player is never overwhelmed by enemies from 360 degrees, but rather faces "funneled" challenges that require them to use cover and physics objects effectively.

A key design moment is the central generator room, which acts as a "Valve" (a point of no return). Here, the player must defend against a multi-directional siege before escaping, utilizing the spatial awareness built up during the earlier, slower-paced exploration segments.
